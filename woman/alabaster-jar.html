The Alabaster Jar

She broke the vessel of her past,
And poured it out on Love at last.
No Pharisee could see within,
That love itself undoes the sin.

For sin is but a thought once kept,
A state assumed, where sorrow wept;
But faith in Love creates anew—
The self forgiven, pure, and true.

Your Sins Are Forgiven

"Then turning toward the woman he said to Simon, “Do you see this woman? I entered your house; you gave me no water for my feet, but she has wet my feet with her tears and wiped them with her hair.
You gave me no kiss, but from the time I came in she has not ceased to kiss my feet.
You did not anoint my head with oil, but she has anointed my feet with ointment.
Therefore I tell you, her sins, which are many, are forgiven—for she loved much. But he who is forgiven little, loves little.”
And he said to her, “Your sins are forgiven.”
Then those who were at table with him began to say among themselves, “Who is this, who even forgives sins?”
And he said to the woman, “Your faith has saved you; go in peace.” — Luke 7:44-50
